About the BRFSS Dataset

Understanding the data behind public health decisions

What is BRFSS?

The Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS) is a health-related telephone survey conducted by the CDC in collaboration with U.S. states and territories. Since 1984, it has collected data on health behaviors, chronic conditions, and healthcare access from adults aged 18 and older.

What Was Surveyed in 2023?

  • Health Status & Behaviors: Exercise, tobacco use, alcohol consumption
  • Chronic Conditions: Hypertension, diabetes, long-term COVID effects
  • Preventive Measures: Immunizations, cancer screenings
  • Social Determinants: Social factors affecting health outcomes

How the Data Was Collected

Sampling Method: The survey used both landline and cellular telephone sampling to reach a diverse adult population across all U.S. states and territories.

Data Collection: Interviews were conducted 7 days a week using Computer-Assisted Telephone Interview (CATI) systems. Trained interviewers asked participants standardized questions about their health.

Data Processing: The CDC weighted responses to ensure the data accurately represents the U.S. adult population, accounting for demographic differences and sampling design.

Understanding the Data Terms

Frequency: The number of respondents who selected a particular answer

Percentage: The proportion of respondents who selected an answer

Weighted Percentage: Adjusted percentage that accounts for sampling design, making the data more representative of the actual population

Why This Data Matters

State health departments and the CDC use BRFSS data to:

  • Identify health disparities across different populations
  • Design and evaluate public health programs
  • Address emerging health issues
  • Propose health-related legislation
  • Measure progress toward health objectives
